How I Think

Constraints, Reframed.

Architecture constraints are just problems that haven't been reframed yet. Two more decisions worth seeing — plus a follow-up artifact on forensic signal extraction. Forensic Payload ↗

DECISION 02
Why Typed Output Contracts?

The enrichment JSON was nondeterministic — different key names, different nesting, different field locations every run. The normalization layer had seven fallback paths just to find an email. The fix: an output contract at the stage boundary. The model treats it as ground truth. Normalization collapsed from seven paths to one.

DECISION 03
Why The Parser Layer?

The practical problem was not the language or framework. It was messy payloads moving between tools. The parser layer normalized n8n input, stripped bad URLs and citation noise, and made each row usable before it reached the next stage.

The Person

The Handoff Matters More Than The Model.

I learned that the hard part of AI-assisted GTM work is rarely the model by itself. It is the handoff: what each step receives, what it is allowed to output, and whether the next tool can trust the payload.

This experiment wasn't built to be impressive. It was built to be understood. I rebuilt Clay-like enrichment logic in plain text, tested output contracts at each boundary, and learned which parts belong inside Clay columns, which parts belong in HubSpot, and which parts should never reach an outbound tool until QA passes.

I sit at the intersection of revenue operations, marketing automation, and AI-assisted workflow design — which means I care about clean CRM payloads, HubSpot handoffs, and practical Clay tables as much as clever prompts.
